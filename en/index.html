What this is

Big Tech Files is a reference portal documenting 33 cases of privacy, security and ethics violations by the largest technology companies — Meta, Google, Apple, Microsoft, Amazon, Uber, TikTok, X (formerly Twitter), Clearview AI, Zoom and LinkedIn.

Each case is a documentary card based exclusively on regulatory decisions, court documents, investigative journalism, academic research and whistleblower disclosures. All amounts and dates are verified against at least two independent sources.

Five doors into the data

The portal has no single narrative path. The homepage presents five equal entries into the same 33 cases:

  • WHAT — grid of 33 cases with filters and full-text search
  • WHEN — narrative timeline 2010–2026
  • WHERE — map of regulators and jurisdictions
  • WHY — network graph linking cases, people, companies
  • WHO REVEALED IT — gallery of 12 whistleblowers and journalists

Privacy of this portal

A project that documents privacy violations cannot itself violate the privacy of its readers. Matryca collects no user data, uses no analytics cookies, loads no external trackers. All assets are self-hosted.
