Who revealed it

None of the 33 cases documented in Big Tech Files came to light by itself. Each was forced into the public record by specific people doing specific work at specific moments. This gallery is a minimum map of that labour.

Full biographies (~1,500 words each) are available in the Polish edition. English short profiles follow. Full translations planned for Phase 4.

  • 2021

    Whistleblower — Meta

    Frances Haugen

    Harvard Business School graduate and former product manager on Facebook's Civic Integrity team. Leaked 22,000 pages of internal documents. Testified before the US Senate, UK, EU. Her disclosures forced a global debate about Instagram's harm to teen girls and Facebook's XCheck VIP-exemption system.

  • 2011–

    Lawyer, activist — NOYB

    Max Schrems

    Austrian lawyer who, as a Vienna law student in 2011, filed the first formal complaint against Facebook Ireland. His cases brought down Safe Harbor (2015) and Privacy Shield (2020). Founded NOYB in 2017; has since won over €2 billion in European enforcement decisions.

  • 2018

    Whistleblower — Cambridge Analytica

    Christopher Wylie

    28-year-old Canadian with pink hair and a PhD in fashion trend forecasting. Former research director at Cambridge Analytica. His two-hour interview in Carole Cadwalladr's London flat became the foundation of the March 2018 Observer/NYT series.

  • 2018–

    Journalist — The Observer

    Carole Cadwalladr

    British journalist whose investigations revealed Cambridge Analytica, Russian influence via Facebook in the Brexit referendum, and the mechanics of personalized political targeting. Pulitzer finalist, TED speaker, defendant in defamation cases she ultimately won.

  • 2020–

    Journalist — New York Times

    Kashmir Hill

    NYT technology reporter whose January 2020 article "The Secret Company That Might End Privacy as We Know It" exposed Clearview AI. Has since documented face-recognition wrongful-arrest cases and smart-device surveillance in domestic abuse contexts.

  • 2022

    Whistleblower — Twitter

    Peiter "Mudge" Zatko

    Hacking legend, former DARPA director, former Twitter head of security. Filed SEC/FTC/DOJ disclosure in August 2022 alleging Twitter had "extreme, egregious" security vulnerabilities. Testified before the US Senate. His claims about bot counts became a central argument in Musk's attempt to exit the Twitter acquisition.

  • 2013

    Whistleblower — NSA

    Edward Snowden

    NSA contractor who disclosed to journalists 1.5 million pages of documents in June 2013. Revealed PRISM, MUSCULAR, and the full architecture of mass surveillance. His disclosures triggered GDPR passage (2016), Schrems I (2015), Schrems II (2020). Lives in Moscow.

  • 2010

    Regulator — Hamburg

    Johannes Caspar

    Lawyer and then-Hamburg Data Protection Commissioner. His three-year pursuit of Google Street View forced the 14 May 2010 disclosure that Street View cars had collected Wi-Fi traffic fragments in 30+ countries. First credible enforcement action against a US tech giant by a European regional regulator.

  • 2017

    Whistleblower — Uber

    Susan Fowler

    Former Uber site-reliability engineer. Her February 2017 blog post "Reflecting on One Very, Very Strange Year at Uber" went viral. Within five months, CEO Travis Kalanick was forced out. Catalyst for #MeToo in tech.

  • 2016–

    Journalist — NYT, BuzzFeed

    Ryan Mac

    Investigative journalist covering Meta, Uber, ByteDance. Co-author of the BuzzFeed exposé revealing Beijing-based ByteDance staff accessing US TikTok user data.

  • 2022–

    Journalist — Forbes, BuzzFeed

    Emily Baker-White

    Reporter whose leaked internal TikTok recordings (June 2022) confirmed US employees saying "everything is seen in China". Forced the "Project Texas" data-segregation program and congressional hearings.

  • 2013–

    Journalist — New York Times

    Paul Mozur

    Pulitzer-winning NYT reporter covering technology and authoritarianism. Documented Chinese government surveillance of Uyghurs via facial recognition and Xinjiang police databases. Forced into exile from Hong Kong.
