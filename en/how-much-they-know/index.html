Questionnaire — fifteen topics

How much they know

Fifteen quick questions, one for each category of data that Big Tech can — and in many cases does — hold on you. Each question is linked to at least one documented case in the database.

Interactive questionnaire with personal risk scoring is currently available in the Polish edition. English topic list follows. The full interactive version is planned for Phase 4.

  1. 01

    Location

    Do any apps have access to your precise location?

    Even "Never" settings often leak IP-based geolocation. Google kept tracking users who had turned Location History off — the 2022 multistate settlement cost $391.5M.

  2. 02

    Microphone

    Is your microphone always available to an app running in the background?

    Siri, Alexa, Google Assistant all listen for wake words. In 2019 it emerged that Apple contractors were grading intimate fragments of these recordings — including medical visits and sexual encounters.

  3. 03

    Camera

    Have you ever checked which apps have permission to use your camera?

    In 2019 TikTok was fined $5.7M (FTC) for collecting children's images. In 2023 Amazon paid $5.8M because Ring doorbell employees watched video of customers.

  4. 04

    Contacts

    Do you know which apps can read your contacts?

    WhatsApp (2021), LinkedIn (2020) and dozens of others automatically scrape your contact list. Information about people who never signed up for the service ends up in Meta and Microsoft databases.

  5. 05

    Ad targeting

    Have you seen an ad that felt "too personalized"?

    Google Location History kept running after users turned it off (2018). Meta served ads across websites without consent until the €390M Irish DPC fine in January 2023.

  6. 06

    Face

    Have you ever uploaded a photo of your face to a public network?

    Clearview AI has scraped 50+ billion face photos from Facebook, LinkedIn, Instagram. Sold to 2,400+ US police agencies. Fines in France, Italy, Greece, UK — all unpaid.

  7. 07

    Health

    Does your app track your steps, sleep, heart rate?

    Period-tracking apps have been subpoenaed by US prosecutors post-Roe. Health data, unlike medical records, is not protected by HIPAA when collected by non-medical apps.

  8. 08

    Financial

    Do financial apps have access to your purchase history?

    In 2012 Target predicted customers' pregnancies from purchase patterns. Known case: a father in Minneapolis learned of his teenage daughter's pregnancy from a Target catalogue.

  9. 09

    Browser history

    Do you know that "incognito" mode does not hide you from your ISP, employer, or visited sites?

    Google Chrome Incognito was silently still logged by Google ad tech. Brown v. Google settled in 2023 — estimated $5 billion.

  10. 10

    E2E myth

    Do you assume "end-to-end encryption" means your messages cannot be read by the platform?

    Zoom advertised "E2EE" that did not exist. Keys stored on Zoom servers. Part of traffic routed through China. $85M class action (2021).

  11. 11

    Children

    Do you know what age must be verified before a child uses an app?

    TikTok made child accounts public by default. "Family Pairing" allowed adults to message minors without verification. €345M Irish DPC fine (September 2023).

  12. 12

    Deletion

    Have you tried to delete data and checked whether it was actually deleted?

    Amazon stored Alexa voice recordings indefinitely, even after deletion requests, including children's recordings. $25M FTC fine (2023).

  13. 13

    Data breach

    Has any service you use ever suffered a data breach?

    533 million Facebook phone numbers leaked in 2021 (2.67 million Polish users). Equifax: 147 million Americans (2017). Most users never receive individual notice.

  14. 14

    Export

    Have you ever requested a GDPR data export from a major service?

    Max Schrems requested 1,200 pages from Facebook in 2011 as a 23-year-old student. His single request triggered Schrems I, Schrems II, and €2B+ in subsequent fines.

  15. 15

    AI training

    Do you know which of your data was used to train AI models?

    LinkedIn silently activated AI training on user posts and messages in 2024. Opt-out, not opt-in. €310M fine from Irish DPC. Meta, xAI (Grok), Gemini — all doing similar things.
