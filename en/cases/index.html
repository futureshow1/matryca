33 documented cases

Cases

Each case card is a reference document based exclusively on regulatory decisions, court records, investigative journalism, academic research and whistleblower disclosures. All figures and dates are verified against at least two independent sources.

Full case cards (3,000–4,000 words each) are currently available in the Polish edition. English summaries are listed below. Click a title to open the Polish card — the source notes and citations are largely language-independent.

  • A01 2025

    Local Mess

    Meta

    Meta and Yandex apps covertly used localhost sockets to re-identify incognito browser sessions. Eight years of practice. Three hours to switch off after publication.
  • A02 2018

    Cambridge Analytica

    Meta

    87 million Facebook profiles harvested via a personality-test app. Psychographic targeting in Trump 2016 and Brexit. $5B FTC fine. Stock went up.
  • A03 2019

    Onavo / Facebook Research / Project Atlas

    Meta

    Facebook paid teenagers $20/month for root access to their phones via a "research VPN" that bypassed Apple and Google app store rules.
  • A04 2021

    Leak of 533M phone numbers

    Meta

    533 million Facebook phone numbers scraped via a contact-import flaw. 2.67 million Polish users among them. Meta declined to notify affected users.
  • A05 2021

    Facebook Files / Frances Haugen

    Meta

    22,000 internal pages leaked by Frances Haugen. Instagram documented as harmful to teen girls. Senate testimony, SEC filings, EU disclosures.
  • A06 2014

    Emotional contagion experiment

    Meta

    Facebook manipulated feeds of 689,003 users for one week without informed consent. Published in PNAS. Sparked the ethics debate about platform experiments.
  • A07 2023

    Meta — €1.2B DPC fine

    Meta

    Record GDPR fine for transferring EU user data to the US without legal basis after Schrems II. Stock price rose the week after.
  • A08 2023

    New Mexico v. Meta

    Meta

    New Mexico AG sued Meta for exposing children to sexual content and predators. Internal documents revealed Meta knew for years and did nothing.
  • A09 2023

    €390M fine — behavioral ads

    Meta

    Meta cannot automatically serve behavioral ads based on "contract performance". Must ask for consent. Nine-year legal journey by Max Schrems.
  • A10 2025

    €200M DMA fine

    Meta

    First penalty under the EU Digital Markets Act. Meta's "pay or consent" subscription model ruled incompatible with free choice.
  • B01 2010

    Google Street View Wi-Spy

    Google

    Street View cars collected fragments of open Wi-Fi traffic — emails, passwords, URLs — in 30+ countries for three years. Hamburg regulator forced disclosure.
  • B02 2012

    Google Safari workaround

    Google

    Google circumvented Safari's default cookie block via a hidden form trick. $22.5M FTC fine — at the time, largest privacy fine in US history.
  • B03 2018

    Google+ API bugs

    Google

    Two API bugs exposed private data of ~500,000 users (2015) and 52.5 million (2018). Google chose not to disclose. $7.5M class-action settlement.
  • B04 2018

    Google Location History

    Google

    Google continued tracking users even after they turned Location History off. AP investigation, 40-state settlement, $391.5 million.
  • B05 2020

    Brown v. Google

    Google

    Class action over Chrome Incognito mode secretly still being tracked by Google. Settled in 2023 — details sealed, estimated $5 billion.
  • B06 2018

    Project Dragonfly

    Google

    Secret Google project to build a censored search engine for China. Exposed by The Intercept. Internal revolt by engineers. Project officially dead in 2019.
  • C01 2017

    Apple "Batterygate"

    Apple

    Apple secretly throttled older iPhones. $500M US class action, $25M French CNIL fine, €10M Italian Antitrust.
  • C02 2019

    Apple Siri grading

    Apple

    Apple contractors listened to intimate Siri recordings — medical appointments, sex, business calls — for quality grading. No opt-in, no notice.
  • C03 2021

    Apple CSAM scanning

    Apple

    Apple announced on-device scanning for child sexual abuse material. EFF and cryptographers warned: the infrastructure is reusable for dissident surveillance. Apple backed down in three weeks.
  • C04 2024

    Microsoft Recall

    Microsoft

    Windows 11 feature took screenshots every few seconds. Security researchers demonstrated plaintext extraction. Microsoft delayed launch indefinitely.
  • C05 2024

    LinkedIn AI training

    LinkedIn / Microsoft

    LinkedIn quietly activated AI training on user posts and messages. Opt-out, not opt-in. Irish DPC — €310 million fine.
  • D01 2023

    Amazon Ring

    Amazon

    Ring doorbell employees watched video recordings of customers for years. Police accessed footage without warrants. $5.8M FTC fine.
  • D02 2019

    Amazon Alexa recordings

    Amazon

    Amazon stored Alexa recordings indefinitely, even after user deletion. Employees transcribed samples. Class actions across multiple states.
  • D03 2023

    Amazon Alexa COPPA

    Amazon

    Amazon retained children's voice recordings indefinitely in violation of COPPA. $25M FTC fine.
  • D04 2014

    Uber God View

    Uber

    Uber employees used "God View" tool to track journalists, celebrities, ex-partners in real time. $20K New York AG settlement — for abuse that should have cost millions.
  • D05 2017

    Uber Greyball

    Uber

    Software tool to identify and deceive regulators and police. Deployed in Portland, Paris, Boston, Philadelphia. Exposed by NYT.
  • D06 2017

    Uber 2016 breach

    Uber

    Hacker stole data of 57 million users and drivers. Uber paid hacker $100,000 to conceal the breach. CSO Joe Sullivan convicted of obstruction in 2022.
  • E01 2020

    TikTok / ByteDance

    TikTok

    TikTok algorithm, addictive design, data flows to China. Multiple investigations worldwide.
  • E02 2023

    TikTok — children

    TikTok

    Child accounts were public by default. "Family Pairing" allowed adults to message minors without verification. €345M Irish DPC fine.
  • E03 2022

    TikTok — data transfers to China

    TikTok

    Internal TikTok recordings confirmed Beijing-based ByteDance staff accessing US user data. Emily Baker-White reporting. Ongoing proceedings.
  • E04 2024

    X / Grok Imagine

    X / xAI

    xAI launched image generator without safety filters. Taylor Swift deepfakes, political fakes, violence. EU DSA investigation.
  • E05 2020

    Clearview AI

    Clearview AI

    50+ billion face photos scraped from the public internet. Sold to police. Massive European fines — all unpaid. Firm maintains it is outside GDPR reach.
  • E06 2020

    Zoom — end-to-end myth

    Zoom

    Zoom marketed "E2EE" that did not exist. Keys stored on Zoom servers. Traffic routed through China. $85M class-action settlement.
