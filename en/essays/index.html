Editorial framing — four essays

Essays

The case cards are documentary. The essays are interpretive. Four voices — the project authors, a lawyer, an organisation working on digital rights, and a private citizen — each read the same 33-case database from a different angle.

Full essays (~10,500 words total) are currently available in Polish only. English translations planned for Phase 4. Teasers below.

  • ~2,400 words

    From the authors

    Why a reference portal, why in Polish first, why now. A database that documents privacy violations cannot itself violate privacy. The design choices behind Matryca — no analytics, no cookies, no external trackers — are part of the method, not a decoration.

    Read in Polish →

  • ~3,100 words

    From a lawyer

    GDPR is not a paper. The legal architecture behind enforcement — why Irish DPC is the choke point, why Schrems I and Schrems II are structurally different, how "dark patterns" became a recognised legal category. A practitioner's view of what the case-base shows about European administrative law.

    Read in Polish →

  • ~2,800 words

    From an organization

    Written by Panoptykon Foundation — Poland's principal civil-liberties organisation focused on digital rights. Why individual cases require organisational response. How NOYB, EDRi, EFF, Panoptykon split the enforcement burden. What a single citizen realistically can and cannot do.

    Read in Polish →

  • ~2,200 words

    From a citizen

    From the user side. How to read a case card without becoming despairing or cynical. What concretely can you change in your week. What is worth changing and what is performative. Written as a companion to the "citizen conclusions" section of each case card.

    Read in Polish →
